Bitcoin Mining Electronic Waste ‘Growing Threat to Environment’, Says Study

Mining for bitcoin generates substantial electronic waste that "represents a growing threat to the environment", according to a recent study. A co-author of the report told AFP on Friday that the average life cycle of the powerful computers used to unearth the units of the world's leading cryptocurrency was only 1.3 years.

The public returns to the stadiums: they will ask that the fans be vaccinated to enter

After a year and a half with empty stadiums in Argentine football, next weekend the public will return to the fields. This Tuesday, Matías Lammens, Minister of Tourism and Sports, anticipated that the definitive protocol for the return of the public will state that it will be necessary for fans to prove that they are vaccinated, at least with a dose of the inoculant against covid-19.

The government announced that as of Monday meat exports to China will be released

The Minister of Agriculture, Livestock and Fisheries, Julián Domínguez, announced that as of next Monday, exports of canned and manufactured cows to China will be released, in a press conference held after the meeting he held with the Liaison Table. "It is being exported with absolute normality and the remaining problems began to be solved," said Domínguez, accompanied by the Chief of Cabinet, Juan Manzur, and by Governor Omar Perotti.

He defended himself against a robbery and killed the thief: he left a knife stuck in his chest

A man who defended himself from a robbery ended up murdering the criminal with a knife, who tried to rob him during the early hours of this Monday in the town of Villa Celina, in the Buenos Aires district of La Matanza. The victim had been released from jail a short time before. Police officers arrived at the scene after a 911 call made around three in the morning, and once there they found the body of a 20-year-old boy, with a knife stuck in his chest.
